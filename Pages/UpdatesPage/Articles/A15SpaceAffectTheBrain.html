How Long Does Space Affect the Brain? NASA’s Study on Vision & Cognition

Astronauts return to Earth with more than just mission experience—they bring back physiological changes that could last for years. From brain fluid shifts to vision impairment, microgravity fundamentally alters human neurology, cognition, and ocular health. But how long do these changes last?

A NASA-led study is tracking astronauts post-flight and for up to 5 years after landing to measure long-term structural and functional changes in the brain and eyes. Using MRI, Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT), and Visual Field Analysis, researchers aim to understand whether the neurological effects of spaceflight are permanent or reversible.

Key Goals of the Study:
  1. Quantify neuro-ocular changes up to 5 years post-flight
  2. Determine if astronauts experience lasting cognitive effects.
  3. Develop countermeasures for deep-space missions (including the Moon, Mars & beyond).

As we prepare for long-duration missions, understanding how space alters human cognition and vision is crucial. The ability to process information, navigate, and make real-time decisions depends on neurological health—and that’s exactly what this study is uncovering.
