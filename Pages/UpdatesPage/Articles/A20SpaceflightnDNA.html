Spaceflight & DNA – Understanding Mutation Risks in Microgravity

As space missions extend beyond Earth’s orbit, understanding the long-term health risks of microgravity and radiation is critical. The NASA Twins Genome Study is shedding light on how space alters DNA—particularly somatic mutations, which can impact neurological, cardiovascular, and cancer risks.

What the study found:
  1. Higher mutation accumulation in astronauts compared to Earth-based controls.
  2. Oxidative DNA damage is linked to increased mutation rates.
  3. Potential biomarker discovery for disease prediction and monitoring.
Why this matters:
  1. Genetic monitoring could redefine astronaut health protocols for Moon & Mars missions.
  2. Findings could lead to new approaches for radiation protection and personalized medicine.
  3. The study opens a new field of space-based genetic risk assessment.

Genomic research will be crucial in understanding and mitigating long-term health risks. Could personalized medicine and AI-driven risk assessments be the key to ensuring astronaut longevity in deep space?
