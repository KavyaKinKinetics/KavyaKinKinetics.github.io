Can Nutrition Protect Astronaut DNA from Space-Induced Stress?

Astronauts endure intense physical and oxidative stress, leading to DNA damage and genomic instability—a major challenge for long-duration missions. But new research suggests antioxidant-rich diets may help enhance DNA repair and reduce mutation risks.

Key findings from the study:
  1. DNA repair efficiency improves in individuals with higher antioxidant levels.
  2. Cold Pressor Test (CPT) experiments showed that oxidative stress influences DNA repair gene expression.
  3. Nutrients like Lutein, β-Carotene, and Vitamin E may counteract space-induced genomic damage.
Why this matters for spaceflight & health:
  1. It could be a nutritional countermeasure for astronauts facing oxidative stress.
  2. Findings may help develop dietary interventions for ageing, cancer prevention, and metabolic disorders.
  3. Reinforces the role of nutrition in DNA stability and human longevity.

As we explore deeper into space, DNA stability will be as crucial as cardiovascular and muscular health. Could AI-driven personalized nutrition plans optimize astronaut well-being and extend health spans on Earth?
