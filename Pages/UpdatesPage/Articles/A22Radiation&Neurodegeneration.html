Protecting Astronaut Brains from Radiation & Neurodegeneration

Space travel exposes astronauts to radiation, oxidative stress, and neuroinflammation—risk factors that can accelerate cognitive decline and neurodegeneration. But what if RNA-based therapeutics could prevent these effects?

Researchers at Sachi Bio, in collaboration with NASA, are developing NIT112, a novel RNA therapy designed to:
  1. Target neuroinflammation and reduce Tau pathology (linked to Alzheimer’s).
  2. Enhance cognitive resilience in preclinical models.
  3. Act as a potential countermeasure against space radiation-induced brain damage.
Why this matters for space & Earth:
  1. It could protect astronauts’ cognitive function during long-duration missions.
  2. Has potential applications in treating neurodegenerative diseases like ALS & Alzheimer’s.
  3. Advances personalized brain health strategies using RNA-based medicine.

As we prepare for longer space missions, solutions that protect astronaut cognition and long-term health will be critical. Could RNA therapies revolutionize both space medicine and neurodegenerative care on Earth?
