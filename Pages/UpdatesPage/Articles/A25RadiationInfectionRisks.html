Can Space Radiation Increase Infection Risks?

Astronauts face a double threat in space—not just radiation exposure but also increased infection risks due to weakened immune function in microgravity. A new study from Arizona State University & NASA investigates how radiation affects host-pathogen interactions, specifically with Salmonella bacteria in 3D human tissue models.

Key findings from the study:
  1. Radiation exposure increased bacterial invasion & survival, suggesting higher infection risks in astronauts.
  2. Immune system changes in space could make infections more severe than on Earth.
  3. Researchers are testing radioprotective compounds as potential infection countermeasures.
Why this matters for space & Earth:
  1. Future long-duration missions need antimicrobial strategies to protect astronaut health.
  2. Could inform infection control in immunocompromised patients on Earth.
  3. Highlights the need for AI-driven immune monitoring & countermeasure development.
