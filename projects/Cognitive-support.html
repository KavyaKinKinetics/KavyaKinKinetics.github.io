KinKinetics had the exceptional opportunity to participate in the 20th NeuroPsychoEconomics Conference held in Milan this June. This prestigious event was a convergence of leading minds exploring the intersection of neuroscience, psychology, and economics, providing profound insights into cognitive support mechanisms.

One of the highlights was an enlightening talk by Thomas Zoëga Ramsøy, CEO and founder of Neurons Inc. He offered invaluable insights into the practical applications of consumer neuroscience, delving deep into consumer behavior and the underlying neural processes. His expertise shed light on how understanding the brain's workings can enhance marketing strategies and consumer engagement.

Another remarkable presentation was delivered by Professor Benjamin Scheibehenne from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) in Germany. As a full professor of cognition and cognitive decision-making, he explored how individual behaviors influence economic decisions. His talk was truly mind-blowing, providing a comprehensive analysis of the cognitive factors that drive economic outcomes.

Being part of this significant event was incredibly inspiring for us at KinKinetics. The insights and knowledge gained have been instrumental in advancing our cognitive support technologies. The conference reinforced our commitment to enhancing decision-making processes, particularly in understanding how cognitive fatigue can impact performance. The experience was not only educational but also reaffirmed the importance of integrating neuroscience insights into our work.

Engaging with global experts during the networking sessions at the conference enriched our understanding of cognitive support applications. The collaborative discussions sparked new ideas for integrating neuroscience insights into our AI systems.

The state-of-the-art venue in Milan provided an inspiring backdrop for the exchange of cutting-edge research and innovative concepts in neuropsychoeconomics.

Our team was proud to represent KinKinetics at this influential event. The collective experiences and knowledge gained are pivotal in advancing our mission to enhance cognitive support technologies.
